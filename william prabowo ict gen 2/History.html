Is the moon landing fake?

No, the moon landing was not fake. Scientific, historical, and third-party evidence confirms that NASA's Apollo missions landed on the Moon between 1969 and 1972. Claims that it was a hoax are widely regarded by experts as conspiracy theories, with evidence including lunar photographs from other nations' space agencies. Evidence Confirming the Moon Landing: Third-Party Verification: In 2008, the Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA) took photos of the Apollo 15 landing site, confirming the presence of landing gear and rover tracks. Lunar Samples: Astronauts brought back 382 kilograms (842 pounds) of rocks, core samples, and soil that have been verified by scientists globally as being of lunar origin.
