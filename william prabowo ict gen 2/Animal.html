Bees operate as a highly organized social colony, divided into a queen (reproduction) and female worker bees, who manage all hive tasks like foraging, nursing, and building honeycomb. They produce honey by collecting nectar in a special stomach, passing it to other bees, and fanning it dry, while simultaneously pollinating plants by transferring pollen on their bodies.
